The Rare Roadmap — 12-Month Prodigy Arc

Parallel with JEE part-time. One primary resource per block. Weekly output: one recorded mix, one filmed clip, or one transcribed worship song.
Phase 0 · Weeks 1–4Hear Before You Touch Gear15 min/day ear · 20 min theory
Daily: musictheory.net exercises → Functional Ear Trainer (scale degrees in key). Read: Open Music Theory ch.1–4 (intervals, triads, diatonic harmony). Guitar: JustinGuitar Grade 1 + learn every note on low E and A strings. Goal: hear I–V–vi–IV in any worship song before looking at chords.
✓ Output: transcribe 4 worship songs by ear into Nashville numbers only (no chord names)
Phase 1 · Months 2–3Fretboard + Worship Harmony30 min guitar · 15 min NNS
Guitar: JustinGuitar CAGED + Music Theory modules. Practice capo logic and transposing on PraiseCharts free previews. Theory: Hooktheory I (visual chord function). Worship: study why "build" = IV or V, why pre-chorus lifts to vi or ii. Map word → chord change on 10 Hillsong/Elevation tracks.
✓ Output: lead one song at church with NNS chart + intentional voicings (no root-position strumming)
Phase 2 · Months 3–5Signal Flow & Virtual Consolegaming laptop lab
Read: Recording Revolution gain staging series → Sound On Sound fundamentals. Hands-on: install X32-Edit + Reaper (free) — route virtual channels, practice gain staging, HPF, EQ, compression, aux sends (pre vs post). Mics: Shure Insights full polar-pattern hub. Draw your church's signal chain on paper.
✓ Output: diagram + 500-word doc explaining every knob on one channel strip
Phase 3 · Months 4–6DAW Production (Reaper → Ableton)MIDI keyboard required
Start Reaper (free, unlimited) — record Epiphone DI, layer MIDI pads from keyboard. Then Ableton Live trial — session view for loop-based worship pads. Coursera: Berklee "Music Production" + "Developing Your Musicianship." FL Studio: optional for beat design; not primary for worship.
✓ Output: recreate one Elevation/Bethel stem layout (drums, bass, pads, arp) in MIDI + one live-ready click track
Phase 4 · Months 5–7Live Church Sound + IEMsvolunteer at booth
Read weekly: ProSoundWeb Church Sound. Practice: Mixing Station tutorials. Study feedback, ring-out, drum mic placement (ChurchLeaders guide). Shadow your church FOH — run scene recall, don't touch master fader first month. MultiTracks University for real pro walkthroughs.
✓ Output: mix one full Sunday multitrack from MxU or Churchfront free stems
Phase 5 · Months 6–9Stream Stack (OBS → ATEM → NDI)iPhone as cam A
OBS on laptop: scenes, audio routing, NDI input. Blackmagic Camera app on iPhone — manual exposure, 180° shutter, white balance. ProPresenter trial + NDI Tools. Study Ruah Creative House tiered church setups. Wire: room audio → interface → OBS (not iPhone mic).
✓ Output: 10-min mock service stream (3 scenes, lyrics lower-third, room audio)
Phase 6 · Months 8–12DaVinci + iPhone Cinema + Masteringfree Resolve
DaVinci Resolve free — edit page → color page (lift/gamma/gain, scopes). Wolfcrow + StudioBinder for framing. Filmic Pro or Blackmagic Camera for LOG on iPhone. Mastering: iZotope Ozone Elements or Reaper JSFX limiter — learn LUFS targets (-14 stream, -9 worship release). Adobe Audition for podcast/voice cleanup only.
✓ Output: 60-sec cinematic worship B-roll grade + mastered acoustic guitar cover
Always OnSpiritual Leader + CS Brainnon-negotiable
Tech serves the room first, stream second. Study Worship Together leader devos + Tim Keller on vocation. Your CS skills → automate lyric slides, build stream health dashboards, document signal chains in Notion. Monk rule: one output/week — commit, mix, film, or transcribe.
✓ You become the person who can play, hear, mix, film, and shepherd — not just operate gear
Your starter rig (₹0 extra to begin): Gaming laptop → Reaper + OBS + DaVinci Resolve + X32-Edit · Epiphone → free Tuner + JustinGuitar · MIDI keyboard → Reaper/Ableton virtual instruments · iPhone → Blackmagic Camera app + Filmic Pro trial · Coursera → Berklee specializations (you already subscribe)
01 — signal flow, the thing everything else sits on top of
Recording Revolution
RAREFREE
Graham Cochrane's free blog/YouTube — the plainest-English explanation of gain staging, EQ, and compression that exists. Start here before any gear-specific tutorial. Built for beginners who don't know what they don't know yet.
recordingrevolution.com
Sound On Sound — Techniques
RAREFREE
The oldest and most technically respected audio publication online. Their "Mix Rescue" and fundamentals series unpack signal flow, gain staging, and console theory at a depth no YouTube channel matches. Free article archive going back decades.
soundonsound.com
PreSonus Technical Articles
FREE
Manufacturer-written but gear-agnostic in substance — clean explainers on signal chain, aux sends (pre vs post-fader), buses, and VCA groups. Good second-pass reading once Recording Revolution gives you the vocabulary.
presonus.com/learn
02 — microphone theory: what, why, where
Shure Insights / Mic Basics
RAREFREE
Shure practically invented mainstream mic education. Their polar pattern, dynamic-vs-condenser, and proximity-effect explainers are the industry-standard reference — most audio schools cite them. Read every article in the "microphone basics" hub.
shure.com/insights
ChurchLeaders — Mics in Worship (Drums)
RAREFREE
Written by a 20-year worship audio veteran specifically for church techs. A real, usable kick/snare/tom/overhead mic-selection-and-placement guide for a worship kit — not generic studio advice. This is exactly your gear-audit checklist made concrete.
churchleaders.com
Sweetwater inSync — Microphones
FREE
Deep buyer's-guide-style articles that double as theory lessons — large vs small diaphragm, ribbon mics, when phantom power matters. Written to help you choose gear, which means it teaches you the underlying "why" as a side effect.
sweetwater.com/insync
DPA Mic University
RAREFREE
One of the most technically rigorous free mic-theory resources on the internet — polar patterns explained with actual physics, not just diagrams. Goes further than any beginner needs at first, but is the resource to return to once basics click.
dpamicrophones.com/mic-university
